WORD/IMAGE BOOK

November 2025

For this project we began by selecting a word that can be associated with an image. An onomatopeic term, a word that sounds like an effect or action it denotes, of an object, a characteristic, or a descriptor of the object could be used for the word. The type we selected for our word was limited to the classics listed in our textbooks. With a minimum of four letters and the word and image are the visual elements of the project. Our requirements were one page: black/white, one page: black/white/gray, one page: black/white/color, and one page: black/white/gray/color.

Process:

After working with the word swirl I decided on a shell, which I later changed the type of shell I wanted to use to best depict a swirl. This shell that I picked does that with a swirl being the body of it and protruding out to create a swirl like an ice cream cone.

Iterations:

I ended up choosing LTC Caslon Pro Italic for this project out of all the typefaces because of the elements of the w having a swirl or loop through it which best fit my design. One of the mockups I had of the swirl for page 2, was changed because I felt the word could bigger and have a better clipping mask. It also was too busy and didn't depict a swirl to the fullest ability.
